Quick Win Improvements

Three targeted improvements: batch delete for shots, reworked shoot readiness priority badges, and subtle page transitions.

Feature 1

Shot Batch Delete

Adds a destructive "Delete" action to the existing bulk action bar. Soft-deletes selected shots with a confirmation dialog requiring explicit typed confirmation.

UX rationale: The delete button is intentionally placed at the far right, visually separated from non-destructive actions, and uses red styling to signal danger. The typed confirmation ("DELETE") prevents accidental bulk deletions on mobile where taps can be imprecise.

Feature 2

Priority Tag Rework — Shoot Readiness

Replaces the misleading "Low / Medium / High" confidence labels with time-based urgency badges. Products past their launch window are now correctly flagged as OVERDUE, not "Low."

Problem: The current system marks products past their 15-day launch window as "Low" priority. This is backwards — products with imminent or overdue launches should be the most urgent items. The new system uses time-to-launch as the primary signal, with clear urgency labels that match production team mental models.

Feature 3

Page Transitions

All route transitions use a subtle fade + rise animation. CSS-only, no JavaScript required. 200ms ease-out on enter, 150ms ease-in on exit.

UX rationale: Subtle animations create a sense of spatial continuity between pages. The 10px vertical rise gives a grounded, material-like feel without being distracting. Total animation budget is under 200ms to maintain the perception of instant response.
