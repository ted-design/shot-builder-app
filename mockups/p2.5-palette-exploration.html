Direction A Notes
  • Neutral base: zinc (cooler, slightly bluer than stone). Cleaner against white surfaces.
  • Primary button is near-black — high contrast, editorial feel. No competing accent color on every button.
  • Immediate Red (#E31E24) appears only on: logo mark, active sidebar indicator, destructive states. This creates brand moments without visual noise.
  • Sidebar is true black (#09090b) — crisper contrast than current slate-900 (#0f172a). Active bar is red.
  • Shadows are minimal — borders do most of the separation work. Feels like Notion/Linear.
  • Progress bars use near-black — aligns with primary. No rainbow of accent colors.

Direction B Notes
  • Neutral base: slate (same as current). Minimal disruption to existing code.
  • Primary is deep navy (#1e3a5f) — replaces indigo-500. More mature and less "SaaS-y" than indigo. Closer to Linear's palette.
  • Sidebar is very dark navy (#0f1729) — warmer than pure black, more cohesive with the navy primary.
  • Active indicator is blue (#2563eb) — brighter blue for sidebar active state. Provides clear wayfinding.
  • Least visual disruption of the three directions. Slate neutrals stay, only primary and sidebar tones shift.
  • Risk: May still feel too "tech SaaS" for a fashion production tool. The blue family doesn't connect to the Immediate brand identity.

Direction C — Warm Charcoal + Red Accent

Immediate Red (#E31E24) as primary action color. Warm stone neutrals. Charcoal sidebar. The boldest direction — fashion-forward and strongly branded.

Direction C Notes
  • Neutral base: stone (warm undertone). Warmer than slate/zinc, feels less "tech" and more "fashion".
  • Primary is Immediate Red (#E31E24) — the brand color becomes the primary action color. Every CTA button is red. Bold and confident.
  • Sidebar is warm charcoal (#1c1917 / stone-900) — noticeably warmer than slate-900. Pairs well with stone neutrals.
  • "Dark Action" button variant using near-black for secondary prominent actions (non-destructive). Prevents red fatigue.
  • Progress bars are red — strong brand presence throughout the UI. Every interaction reminds you whose tool this is.
  • Risk: Red as primary action may feel aggressive. Users may confuse primary buttons with destructive/error states. Red fatigue is real on content-heavy pages.
  • Mitigation: If chosen, we'd use red sparingly for key CTAs and lean on the dark variant for most actions. Status badge "Shot" uses near-black instead of red to avoid confusion.
